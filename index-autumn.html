September

FRUIT

APPLES

  • Look for unnblemished fruit (minor scuffs are okay!)
  • Check for firmness by gently rotate and squeezing

GRAPEFRUIT

  • Look for plump fruit with smooth skin and a light pink or yellow color
  • Deeper colored fruit tend to have a more intense taste with pinker ones being more sweet and reds more bitter

PEARS

  • Gently press the area around the stem just above the neck; if it is soft it is ripe!
  • If you prefer to eat it in a few days, choose the fruit that are more firm in the same area

POMEGRANATES

  • Choose firm fruit that are more flattened or square shaped rather than round
  • Ripe pomegranates are dark red; deeper colors tend to be sweeter vs. lighter pinks are more underipe and tart

VEGGIES

ASPARAGUS

  • Select bundles with green, firm stalks
  • Avoid those that are limp or dull in color
  • Make sure the tips form tight spears and the base is still healthy

BOK CHOY

  • Look for smooth, crips stalks and green leaves
  • Watch out for yellow spots on leaves

EGGPLANTS

  • Look for smooth and shiny purple skin
  • They should feel heavy and slightly firm with ripe
  • Smaller eggplants tend to be less bitter and have less seeds

TOMATOES

  • Choose round plump tomatoes with smooth skin
  • They should slightly firm and free of blemishes and discoloration
