March

FRUITS

GRAPEFRUIT

  • Look for plump fruit with smooth skin and a light pink or yellow color
  • Deeper colored fruit tend to have a more intense taste with pinker ones being more sweet and reds more bitter

LEMONS

  • Select firm, heavy lemons with bright yellow skin
  • Avoid soft or ‘wrinkled’ fruit
  • Choose thicker skinned lemons if you want lemon zest!

VEGGIES

ASPARAGUS

  • Select bundles with green, firm stalks
  • Avoid those that are limp or dull in color
  • Make sure the tips form tight spears and the base is still healthy

CABBAGES

  • Choose dense and heavy cabbage heads with bright green crisp leaves
  • Watch out for discoloration and softness
  • Pick larger heads for a more mild flavor!

KABOCHA SQUASH

  • Pick heavy squash that are round and have firm green rinds
  • Faint blemishes along the surface are fine!

April

FRUITS

AVOCADOS

  • Choose slightly firm avocados with dark green skin
  • Unripe avocados tend to be a lighter green while overrides ones appear almost black

BLACKBERRIES

  • Ripe blackberries are plump, have dull skin, and a deep purple-blackish color
  • Make sure to check the bottom of the tray for mold!

POMELOS

  • Find heavy fruit with shiny unblemished skin
  • Ripe pomelo will often have a slightly sweet scent

STRAWBERRIES

  • Pick baskets that have bright red, plump berries and fresh green leaves
  • Check for mold on the berries at the bottom

VEGGIES

ASPARAGUS

  • Avoid those that are limp or dull in color
  • Make sure the tips form tight spears and the base is still healthy

CARROTS

  • Look for a round shape with bright coloring and minimal cracks
  • Note that larger carrots tend to be sweeter!

KABOCHA SQUASH

  • Pick heavy squash that are round and have firm green rinds
  • Faint blemishes along the surface are fine!
